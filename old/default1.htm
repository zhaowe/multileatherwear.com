Who we are

Multi Leatherwear Manufacturing Corporation dates back to 1979 as a leather garment manufacturer, when started by Kenneth Tzou in Kaohsiung, Taiwan. Since then, Multi has unabashedly earned a reputation for its quality and service.

Multi moved to Guangzhou, China in 1992 to stay competitive. We have a capacity of 300 workers and 200 sewing machines for its men’s and women’s leather apparel production, most of which is exported to North America, Western Europe, Japan, and Australia. Since the inception of its production in China, Multi has unabashedly maintained its hard-earned reputation for its superb quality, service, and quick turnaround time since its days in Taiwan.

We are equipped with pattern-making scanners, computers, and printers, and high-quality sewing machines as we continue to update our facilities. To meet our growing needs, we are moving to a new factory in the spring of 2005. Despite our modern setup, we leverage close relationships with tanneries in Italy and China to offer competitive prices.

We also work hand-in-hand with our suppliers to develop a diverse array of skins; thereby, creating a vertical integration for our clients, from leather sourcing, product development, production scheduling, to shipping and distribution. In particular, our design competency and manufacturing experience allow us to work closely with clients to develop their products.


Where we are

Headquartered in the Huadu district of Guangzhou, China, we are less than 20 minutes away from the new Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport. We are also 1.5 hours away from Hong Kong by train.

Huadu is nicknamed the “Leather City of China,” and is also home to southern China´s automobile and jewelry industries. Major enterprises include Honda (Japan), Nissan (Japan), and Natural Stone (Taiwan, jewelry manufacturing). More information about Huadu can be found at the government’s website: www.huadu.gov.cn; more information about Guangzhou can be found at: http://www.sinohotelguide.com/cantonfair/information/guangzhou.html.
