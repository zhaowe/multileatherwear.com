Factory History

Multi, a wholly Taiwanese- owned and managed factory, was established in 1979 and moved to Guangzhou, China in 1992.  This relocation happened just as leather garments gained popularity as a fashion item (heavy and almost all men’s leather coats have given or shared way to lightweight, chic leather jackets that can be found in a teenager’s wardrobe).  As a result of both, our business has expanded in both the number of clients and the volume of client’s orders.

With a showroom and experienced merchandisers in HK and an English-speaking merchandising team based in our Guangzhou factory, we strive to be as responsive as we can to minimize potential frustrations of working with an overseas factory.  With our commercial license to import and export, we pass on the savings of a trading company directly to the clients.

Our clients include a wide range of brands, many of them with their own retail stores or distributions in department stores in the US and Western Europe.

Sourcing

Let’s face it.  The bulk of the cost of a leather garment comes from the skins – so sourcing is of paramount importance.  In order to serve our different clientele, our product development team sources from famous tanneries in France, Italy, and Spain, as well as reliable tanneries in domestic China.        

For some of our clients, leather is a novelty test item.  Our team works closely with these clients on product education and development to piece together the right going-to-market leather garment.  These collaborations have been quite successful, resulting in increased volumes and an expanded line of leather garments reaching into different demographic groups across different brands.    For other clients, leather is their main product.  As such, Multi’s strong international and domestic sourcing capability helps these clients find, develop, or make the required skins.  With a history of close to 30 years, we have established a library of skins, and have contracted some foreign designers to spend a few days in the factory.

Quality Control

A sewing machine is a sewing machine is a sewing machine.  But a skilled operator and an experienced factory that understand the different properties and potential blunders of each skin and production are crucial to the success of a production/shipment.

Leather garments, by nature, are a luxury item or part of a higher-end line for most of our clients.  But leather, also by nature, can be inconsistent in its surface and quality.    Therefore, our QC system can start at the first step of production: in our supplier’s tanneries.  We have traveled to Europe and domestic China, respectively in couchette cars and bunked beds, to check the quality of skins before they arrive in our factory.  Once they arrive and have been distributed to our cutters, each cutter is skilled in performing pre-cutting color selections to make sure that color difference is absolutely minimized within a garment. 

Quality Control continues as garments are cut.  A color-checking process is in place to double-check for color difference and skin defects before pieces are transported to the sewing lines. 

                                 

Once at the sewing lines, a production manager circulates among the sewing lines to monitor the completion of a garment via each step.  Such constant supervision and communication among sewing group leaders ensure that the consistency of sewing is maximized among each group.

Attention to Details (Management and Shop Floor)

Our professional and English-speaking merchandisers provide work-in-progress feedback to clients’ teams to ensure smooth bulk productions throughout the process, from pattern-making, cutting, and sewing, to packaging and shipping.  This information and feedback can help us accommodate clients’ last-minute requests.  On the shop floor, most of our operators came to Multi with experience in leather-garment making.  With our on-going training, each operator is very aware and alert of how his or her individual task pieces into the entire garment.  They are trained to give real-time feedback on the production line, always thinking ahead to avoid future problems that might delay productions. 

A case in point: A US client’s production team sent us artwork for a collar button 10 days before delivery date.  Following protocol, our trims buyer double-checked with our English-speaking merchandiser for the spelling of the wording within the artwork.  Finding this client’s typo, we were still able to fix it and still delivered on time.

One-stop shop

We sell finished garments (as opposed to working as a CMT-based factory), thereby  helping clients cut working capital and management costs of sourcing materials and trims themselves.  Our product development team, with a practical knowledge of achievability, makes frequent trips to international trade shows (for styling and skin developments) and local trims markets and specialty-effect factories (wrinkle-effect, laser-cut, floral dyes, computerized embroidery, perforation, beadwork, etc.) to design and merchandise for and with clients, as we are also well set up in proximity and mentality to do. Importantly, we also distinguish ourselves with experienced leather pattern-makers and computerized grading, both of these to lessen the workload on the clients.  Thus, from sourcing, designing and merchandising, to production and packaging, we provide a one-stop shop for our clients.

Management and Social Compliance

At Multi, we work to provide a high-quality working environment for our staff.  This is an important company goal, as well as one demanded by the clients and the competitive Chinese labor market.  We moved to a new factory in August 2005, and are working at present with factory auditors from three brand-name US clients, in order to make us more competitive among suppliers and attractive among workers.

We are a family-owned business, and that spirit is reflected in the sense of camaraderie among the workers.  Visiting clients are welcome to tour the factory and experience the sense of community for themselves.  We are located 15 minutes away from Guangzhou’s new Baiyun Airport , or an hour and a half a train ride away from Hong Kong.
